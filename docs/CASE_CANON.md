# Case Canon

## Case Title

**The Road That Should Have Closed**

## Historical Episode

The Flight to Varennes, June 1791.

## Immutable Mystery Premise

The timeline has fractured somewhere between recognition of the travelers and their detention at Varennes. The player sees several fictional anomaly candidates but is not told which link changed. Fixed, authored observations from the fractured branch show what occurred inside that branch; reviewed historical records show the sequence that must be restored. In the intended fractured branch, Drouet suspects the travelers, follows the announced road toward Verdun, never receives the route correction near Clermont, and fails to reach Varennes before the royal carriage passes the historical interception point.

The player must restore the supported historical sequence:

> recognition or suspicion -> accurate route information -> redirected pursuit -> warning at Varennes -> local mobilization -> (blocked onward passage and passport inspection as parallel local actions) -> guarded collective detention

## Fictional Anomaly Layer

The diagnostic interface exposes three candidate temporal anomalies:

1. **E6A Recognition Echo:** claims that Drouet never suspected the travelers and therefore did not begin a pursuit.
2. **E6B Route Echo:** shows the route correction failing to reach Drouet, so he continues on the announced road toward Verdun rather than redirecting toward Varennes.
3. **E6C Authorization Echo:** claims that Drouet warned Varennes in time, but local actors received an instruction to let the travelers continue.

All three are invented gameplay artifacts and receive the same visible `FICTIONAL TEMPORAL ANOMALY` provenance. None may resemble or be described as an authentic historical document. The evidence title, ordering, color, and tutorial must not reveal which candidate is active.

The player receives three equally labeled, authored `FICTIONAL BRANCH OBSERVATION` records:

- **FO1:** the altered-state Drouet station records that he suspected the travelers and set out on the announced Verdun road.
- **FO2:** the altered Varennes station records that the carriage passed before any warning or civic alarm arrived.
- **FO3:** the altered route replay places Drouet away from Varennes when the carriage passed.

E6B is the intended active fracture. FO1 rejects E6A, while FO2 and FO3 reject E6C and support E6B. Reviewed historical evidence establishes the sequence these branch observations displaced; it cannot by itself determine which fictional alternate cause is active. Fictional branch observations never count as historical corroboration or toward the student's three historical evidentiary lines. The user approved FO1-FO3 and the three FO-dependent gates on 14 July 2026; production implementation must preserve equal anomaly presentation and the fiction/history boundary.

## Correct Hypothesis Family

A repair-ready hypothesis must establish:

1. The player identifies the route-information handoff as the active anomaly by comparing fictional branch observations with reviewed historical evidence, and rejects the recognition and authorization candidates.
2. That corruption delayed or misdirected Drouet's pursuit.
3. Within the authored fracture model, the loss of timely warning prevents the modeled local response. This is a simulation rule, not a verified but-for historical claim.
4. The authored reconstruction requires both passage control and passport inspection to represent the documented collective local response. This is not a historical finding that either action, or their pair, was necessary or sufficient. Drouet alone did not stop or detain the travelers, and the bridge obstruction was not itself the physical arrest.
5. Louis intentionally participated in leaving Paris; his declaration presents the act as seeking liberty and safety, while his complete motives remain open to interpretation.
6. Later official, diplomatic, and radical records framed the flight differently, while the September Constitution still retained monarchy; the deterministic gate does not convert those observations into a universal collapse claim.
7. The event did not single-handedly cause the later fall of the monarchy, republic, or Terror.

## Strong Sample Explanation

The fictional branch observations reject the recognition and authorization echoes and identify route information as the active temporal anomaly; the historical record shows what must be restored. In the sequence targeted by the case, suspicion of the travelers mattered because Drouet learned where the carriage had gone, reached Varennes quickly enough to warn people there, and local residents and officials acted. Within the fictional fracture model, changing that information removes the timely warning; this counterfactual is a gameplay rule rather than a historical certainty. Restoring it repairs one immediate operational link in a larger chain. The arrest's political meaning must be stated with source-backed limits and cannot make later revolutionary outcomes inevitable.

## Plausible Incorrect Hypotheses

### Kidnapping Explanation

Claim:

> Louis was being moved against his will, so the escape was misunderstood.

Why plausible:

- Politically useful contemporary framing.
- Louis can describe himself as seeking liberty and safety.

Why it fails:

- Declaration and independent travel-preparation evidence support Louis's knowing and intentional participation; they do not settle every motive or whether he felt politically constrained.
- It does not explain the route and intended destination.
- It treats political framing as neutral truth.

### Drouet Fabricated His Importance

Claim:

> Drouet later exaggerated or invented his role.

Why plausible:

- His account is retrospective.
- He had reputational incentives.

Why it fails:

- Timing, route, and local-response evidence corroborate central pieces.
- Possible self-presentation does not invalidate every factual claim.

### Missing Escort As Sole Cause

Claim:

> The journey failed only because an escort was absent or coordination failed.

Why plausible:

- Escort and timing problems mattered.

Why it fails:

- It ignores recognition, route information, pursuit, local warning, civic mobilization, passport inspection, and guarded detention.
- It treats one contributing condition as the whole mechanism.

## Required Causal Levels

The Case Brief requires one approved background condition and one approved journey, geographic, or civic condition. These are whitelists, not a requirement to select every item.

### Approved Background Conditions

- `COND-BG-001`: unfinished constitutional settlement and contested political authority
- `COND-BG-002`: the royal family's politically constrained position in Paris
- `COND-BG-003`: Louis's public objection that the emerging settlement left him too little freedom and authority

### Approved Journey, Geographic, Or Civic Conditions

- `COND-JR-002`: accumulated delays and failed or ineffective escort coordination
- `COND-GE-001`: road choices and Varennes's layout constrained pursuit and onward passage
- `COND-CV-001`: municipal officers, residents, and National Guards could organize a collective response

Secret departure is the initial event, not a broader condition. Fear of counterrevolution, a conspicuous traveling party, and compound press/public-opinion claims remain deferred and cannot appear in the deterministic condition selector.

### Immediate Mechanism

- recognition or suspicion
- accurate route information
- redirected pursuit
- warning at Varennes
- local mobilization
- blocked onward passage
- passport inspection
- guarded collective detention

### Political Meaning

- Verified records show competing official, diplomatic, and radical framings after the flight.
- The Cordeliers record documents one radical attack on confidence in kingship; it is not universal opinion.
- The September Constitution retained monarchical government, preventing an immediate-collapse reading.
- Students may argue that the episode weakened credibility or made the settlement more fragile, but those remain bounded historical interpretations evaluated formatively rather than deterministic fact gates.
