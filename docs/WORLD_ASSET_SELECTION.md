## Intent And Gate

Select only technically credible, explicitly CC0-1.0 candidates that can support grounded historical atmosphere without converting visual plausibility into a historical claim. An `accept` decision below means only "eligible for later runtime-intake, SHA-256, ledger, performance, and visual-acceptance work." The candidate-only proof in `docs/licenses/world/` does not authorize runtime use by itself.

The release gates applied here are:

1. Rights must be proven on a current official creator/host or license page. Search snippets, reposts, generic "free" labels, and royalty-free terms are insufficient.
2. Downloaded release files must be explicitly CC0-1.0. There is no license exception for characters, animations, or convenience packs.
3. A principal rig must provide a reusable body and compatible in-place idle/walk/run-equivalent clips without adding a retargeting, texture-optimization, or GLTF repackaging pipeline.
4. Classroom remains subject to 15,000,000 initial encoded bytes, and the progressively loaded district remains subject to 35,000,000 encoded bytes. File bytes below are local payload bytes, not a substitute for the later CDP encoded-transfer measurement.
5. Asset-host descriptions establish creator, license, and technical metadata only. They are not historical sources.
6. Generic bodies, clothing, props, materials, and locations remain bounded presentation. They cannot unlock evidence, satisfy assessment, corroborate testimony, or imply exact appearance or placement.

### Rig compatibility conclusion

The Quaternius chain is the only candidate with an official, technically coherent skeleton family. The base, outfit, and animation files use the same 65 joint names in the same order. Chromium loaded `Male_Peasant.gltf` and `UAL1_Standard.glb` without warnings, and an `AnimationMixer` successfully ran Idle, Walk, and Jog with a stationary root. This demonstrates compatibility without retargeting.

It does not pass the release gate. The only free complete outfits inspected are fantasy/medieval peasant or ranger silhouettes, not grounded 1791 clothing. The smallest inspected male outfit and motion combination is 45,936,249 local bytes, above the complete district cap before the app or environment is counted. The separate superhero base is also unsuitable and has two broken texture references. No credible CC0 dressed option therefore works within this plan without historical costume authoring and a texture/asset optimization pipeline.

## Explicit Exclusions

1. **Portrait likeness:** reject every claim that an available head or body reproduces the investigator, Drouet, Louis XVI, Sauce, Barnave, or any resident. Heads may be generic dramatized role markers only. Sauce and Barnave receive no 3D portrait model.
2. **Exact Varennes facade:** reject every label or placement claiming an external module reproduces a specific Varennes building, roofline, storefront, archive, inn, or house in June 1791.
3. **Exact object placement:** reject every claim that a cart, crate, barrel, sign, furniture vehicle, guard, or resident occupied the rendered location. Decoration is non-evidentiary. A cart or obstruction prop must never encode the sole cause of detention.
4. **All bridge geometry:** reject every bridge model, including the inspected Kenney files `bridge-draw.glb` (5,140 bytes), `bridge-straight-pillar.glb` (14,344), and `bridge-straight.glb` (16,832). The exclusion is geometric, not merely filename-based: no deck, span, arch, rail, water crossing, or extension past the reviewed approach boundary may be selected from any pack.
